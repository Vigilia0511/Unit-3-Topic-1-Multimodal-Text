Core Definition

What is a Multimodal Text?

A text is multimodal when it combines two or more communication systems. These systems are called modes.

Simply put: multimodal texts are materials presented using different modes and media.
Mode vs. Media

Two key terms to distinguish

TermMeaningExample
ModeCommunication system to interpret meaningSound, colors, written words
MediaManner of disseminationTraffic sign, radio, poster
In a "No Parking" sign — the sign itself is the medium; the red border and symbol are the modes.
Important Note

Multimodal ≠ Digital Only

Multimodal text is NOT synonymous with digital. It can be paper-based, live, digital, or transmedia.

Memory Aid

L · V · A · G · S

Linguistic · Visual · Audio · Gestural · Spatial — at least two must be combined to make a multimodal text.

Mode 1

Linguistic

Spoken and written language — vocabulary, structure, and grammar of oral/written language.

Essays · speeches · captions · dialogue · subtitles
Mode 2

Visual

Moving or still images — colors, layouts, screen formats, symbols, shot framings, angle, camera movement, subject movement.

Photos · videos · infographics · diagrams · film
Mode 3

Audio

Volume, pitch, and rhythm of music and sound effects.

Music · voiceover · sound effects · radio broadcasts
Mode 4

Gestural

Movement, facial expression, and body language.

Dance · sign language · acting · gestures on stage
Mode 5

Spatial

Proximity, direction, position of layout, and organization of objects in space.

Page layout · poster design · furniture arrangement
Example — A Film uses all 5

All modes in one text

Dialogue (linguistic) · camera shots (visual) · soundtrack (audio) · actor expressions (gestural) · set design/framing (spatial).

Type 1 — Paper-based

Paper Multimodal Text

Traditional physical texts combining modes on a printed surface.

  • Books, comics, posters
  • Brochures, letters, magazines
Type 2 — Digital

Digital Multimodal Text

Electronic texts delivered through devices or the internet.

  • Slide presentations, e-books, blogs
  • Websites, e-posters, films, video games
Type 3 — Live

Live Multimodal Text

Real-time performances combining modes in a physical space.

  • Stage performances, theater, concerts
  • Speeches, demonstrations, school events
Type 4 — Transmedia

Transmedia Multimodal Text

A story told across multiple media platforms, all working together as one whole.

Example: book + comic + film + web series + video game — all part of the same story.

From the lesson — Anti-smoking campaign:

  • Live stage performance
  • Video uploaded to social media
  • Recorded audio aired on radio
  • Poster in schools and community
Concept 1

ICT and Multimodal Communication

Information and Communications Technology (ICT) offers tools that allow people to create multimodal texts. It changed how we design, negotiate, disseminate, and make meaning.

Concept 2

Modes Work Together

In all communication, we use different modes together. Text, images, and sounds are used to both deliver AND understand a message.

No single mode works alone in a truly effective multimodal text — they reinforce each other.
Concept 3

Audience and Context Awareness

An effective multimodal text always considers:

  • Target audience — Who is it for?
  • Context — Where/how will it be delivered?
  • Purpose — What should the audience do or feel?
The "Time is Ticking" poster uses dark colors, a lung image, and urgent text — all chosen deliberately for smokers as the target audience.
Concept 4

Cause-Oriented Multimodal Presentation

Students must propose a cause-oriented activity and present it using multimodality — combining oral, audio-visual, and/or web-based formats for local and global audiences.

From the Lesson

Anti-smoking poster — "Time is Ticking"

  • Visual — darkened lungs, cigarette bomb, black background
  • Linguistic — "Time is ticking… Stop while you still CAN!!"
  • Spatial — image left, slogan right for dramatic impact
Urgency is created by combining all three modes together for the target audience: smokers.
From the Lesson

No Parking traffic sign

  • Medium — the traffic sign (physical object)
  • Visual mode — red border and crossed-out "P" symbol
  • Linguistic mode — the words "NO PARKING"
